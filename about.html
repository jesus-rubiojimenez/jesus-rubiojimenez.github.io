Biography

Jesús Rubio is a Theoretical Physicist and a Research Fellow. In October 2019 he joined the Quantum Non-equilibrium group at the University of Exeter, which is run by Prof Janet Anders, to work as a Research Associate within the Molecular Mechanics Initiative with her and Prof Frank Vollmer.

In July 2020 he was promoted to Research Fellow. He is currently developing physics-driven techniques to enhance free energy calculations and, more generally, the collection and analysis of data in quantum technologies.

His position at Exeter followed the completion of a PhD in Physics at the University of Sussex. There, he devised optimal strategies for quantum metrology with limited resources. He achieved this by using quantum optics, information and estimation tools. He wrote the thesis Non-asymptotic quantum metrology: extracting maximum information from limited data, which he successfully defended in September 2019. His supervisor was Prof Jacob Dunningham, and he collaborated with Dr Paul Knott regularly.

Between 2013 and 2014 he studied a MSc in Theoretical Physics at the Institute of Theoretical Physics of the Autonomous University of Madrid, with a focus on particle physics and cosmology. His dissertation discussed the toric code model in the context of quantum information theory. Around that time he also collaborated with Prof Alfredo Luis Aina, his former undergraduate supervisor at the Complutense University of Madrid. The aim was to study the relationship between quantum and classical relativistic dynamics using semi-classical techniques.

Last but not least, in 2008 he completed the Spanish Baccalaureate, which gave him the inspiration and basic tools to start the path that would lead him towards his current academic goals.
