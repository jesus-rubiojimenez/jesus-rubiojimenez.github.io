Jesús Rubio is a Research Fellow at the University of Exeter. In October 2019 he joined Prof Janet Anders's Quantum Non-equilibrium group at the University of Exeter, to work as a Research Associate within the Molecular Mechanics Initiative with her and Prof Frank Vollmer. In July 2020 he was promoted to Research Fellow. Currently, he is developing physics-driven techniques to enhance free energy calculations and, more generally, the collection and analysis of data in quantum technologies.

This position followed the completion of his PhD in Physics at the University of Sussex, where he devised optimal strategies for quantum metrology with limited resources. He achieved this by using quantum information and estimation tools. As a result, he wrote the thesis Non-asymptotic quantum metrology: extracting maximum information from limited data, which he successfully defended in September 2019. His supervisor was Prof Jacob Dunningham, and he regularly collaborated with Dr Paul Knott.

Previously, he studied a MSc in Theoretical Physics at the Institute of Theoretical Physics of the Autonomous University of Madrid, with a focus on particle physics and cosmology. His His dissertation discussed the toric code model in the context of quantum information theory. Around that time he also collaborated with Prof Alfredo Luis Aina, his former undergraduate supervisor at the Complutense University of Madrid. This collaboration studied the relationship between quantum and classical relativistic dynamics using semi-classical techniques.

Last but not least, in 2008 he completed the Spanish Baccalaureate, which gave him the inspiration and basic tools to start the path that would lead him towards his current academic goals.
