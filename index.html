I am a Research Fellow at the University of Exeter. I work at the intersection of fundamental physics and probability theory, with a focus on quantum estimation theory.

Current research

I am devising physics-driven techniques to enhance the collection and analysis of data in quantum technologies and other AMO platforms, as well as establishing their mathematical basis. At a fundamental level, the aim is to acquire a fresh perspective on how the notion of change provides the information leading to the laws of physics.

Vision

My professional practice is guided by three key ideas:

  • To get further insight on the foundations of physics, one approach is to investigate how the known laws of physics work in concrete applications
  • Every physical law may be generally formulated on the basis of probabilities
  • Science papers should not only inform, but also teach

What does a theoretical physicist do?

My job is to search for the general principles that govern nature, and to create a mathematical framework for their precise formulation. The motivation behind this is the identification of rational explanations for the natural phenomena that underpin human experience. Creating and understanding are thus my core activities.
