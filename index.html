As a theoretical physicist, my job is to search for the general principles that govern nature, and to give them a mathematical formulation. It is hoped that, by using these, human beings can find rational explanations for the natural phenomena that underpins their experience. Creating and understanding are thus my core activities.

Two key ideas lie at the heart of my professional approach to this:

  • Physical laws can be understood as constraints on probabilities, taking the latter to represent uncertainty
  • Concrete applications are an invaluable source of empirical information, from where fundamental insights can be extracted and generalised

My current focus lies the physical framework of quantum technologies such as sensing, metrology, networks and thermometry. The aim is to gain further insight about the fundamental role that information and change play within nature, using the language of quantum estimation theory.

For a more detailed account of my current activities, exploration of this website is highly encouraged.
