I am a Research Fellow at the University of Exeter. I work at the intersection of fundamental physics and probability theory, with a particular focus on quantum estimation theory.

As a theoretical physicist, my job is to search for the general principles that govern nature, and to create a mathematical framework for their precise formulation. The motivation behind this is the identification of rational explanations for the natural phenomena that underpin human experience. Creating and understanding are thus my core activities.

My professional approach is guided by two key ideas:

  • To get further insight on the foundations of physics, one approach is to investigate how the known laws of physics work in concrete applications
  • Every physical law may be generally formulated on the basis of logical probabilities

Currently I am expanding the theoretical framework of quantum technologies such as sensing, networks and thermometry. The aim is to get a fresh perspective on the fundamental role that information and the notion of change play within physics.

For a detailed account of my activity, exploration of this website is highly encouraged.
